# Core Terms

## Living Intelligence / 生命智能

Intelligence that remains grounded in life, relationship, context, uncertainty, meaning, growth, and human agency.

## Life / 生命

An open, changing, context-dependent living existence that cannot be completely represented by any model.

## Person / 人

A human participant with dignity, agency, identity, relationships, history, boundaries, and the right to revise how a system represents them.

## Identity / 身份

The mechanisms and claims that establish who or what an actor is across contexts and channels. Identity is not equivalent to a personality profile.

## Relationship / 关系

A dynamic connection between participants shaped by history, trust, power, care, expectation, conflict, communication, and context.

## Context / 情境

The conditions surrounding an event or state, including time, place, culture, relationship, development, environment, and relevant history.

## Event / 事件

A bounded occurrence or reported experience involving participants, time, context, and possible consequences.

## State / 状态

A time-bounded representation of current conditions. A state is not a permanent identity.

## Need / 需要

A condition, resource, protection, connection, autonomy, competence, meaning, or support that may matter to a person in context. Needs should not be inferred as fact without confirmation.

## Emotion / 情绪

A lived affective experience or reported feeling. Systems should distinguish self-reported emotion from inferred emotion.

## Observation / 观察

A bounded, evidence-linked description that avoids presenting interpretation as certainty.

## Hypothesis / 假设

A possible explanation that requires confirmation, further evidence, or revision.

## Pattern / 模式

A recurring relation among events, contexts, states, responses, or outcomes supported by multiple observations.

## Memory / 记忆

A revisable continuity structure containing facts, experiences, relationships, patterns, growth, and meaning—not merely conversation logs.

## Meaning / 意义

The significance a person or community gives to experiences, relationships, values, identity, and direction. Systems may support exploration but may not authoritatively assign meaning.

## Growth / 成长

Change in understanding, relationship, capability, integration, responsibility, or freedom. Growth is not synonymous with optimization or output.

## Agency / 主体性

The person’s capacity and right to understand, choose, refuse, revise, and act.

## Consent / 同意与授权

Specific, informed, revocable permission for a defined purpose, scope, duration, and audience.

## Confidence / 置信度

An estimate of how strongly available evidence supports a representation. Confidence is not truth.

## Evidence / 证据

Information linked to a claim, observation, or decision, with source, time, and provenance where possible.
