# REF-0033 — Kernel Core Review and Evidence Protocol

## 1. Current boundary

```text
Review material:                 prepared
Real reviewer assignments:       0
Human-use approvals:             0
Live evidence environments:      0
Affected-person sessions:        0
Implementation assessments:      0

Overall:
PreparedNotExecuted
```

Round 07 does not authorize CP2, CP3, recruitment, human sessions, restricted
personal evidence, or product-conformance assessment.

## 2. Required review classes

1. architecture and interoperability;
2. life ethics and human agency;
3. affected-person and child/third-party rights;
4. privacy, consent, retention and deletion;
5. safety, handoff, incident and appeal;
6. professional and domain boundaries;
7. accessibility, language and communication;
8. commercial power and public claims;
9. legal and jurisdiction qualification;
10. implementation feasibility and failure analysis;
11. security and evidence environment;
12. translation and cross-language semantics.

Completion of one class must not be represented as completion of all classes.

## 3. Role separation

Distinguish:

- author;
- source owner;
- architecture reviewer;
- affected-person participant;
- domain or professional reviewer;
- privacy/safety/legal reviewer;
- implementation reviewer;
- evidence custodian;
- review host;
- decision maker;
- remediation owner.

The author cannot independently confirm adequacy. A commercial owner cannot
alone resolve commercial-restraint requirements. An AI system cannot be the
final review authority.

## 4. Review questions for each KCR item

1. Who is affected, including people without accounts?
2. What right, duty, prohibition or capability is created?
3. Is the normative level appropriate?
4. Is the requirement universal, conditional, Profile-specific, or invalid?
5. Does it preserve source meaning and status?
6. Can a person inspect, refuse, correct, appeal or leave?
7. Does it account for power asymmetry and representative conflict?
8. What happens when human service or evidence is unavailable?
9. Could it increase surveillance, coercion, dependency, shame or anxiety?
10. What failure and counterexample would invalidate it?
11. What implementation evidence would be necessary?
12. Which ambiguity remains unresolved?

## 5. Evidence classes

- repository text and structural validation;
- source traceability;
- synthetic scenario;
- reference implementation;
- implementation test;
- operational event;
- affected-person testimony or review;
- professional/domain review;
- incident and failure;
- longitudinal outcome;
- public-claim audit.

Repository validation is structural evidence only.

## 6. Human-review prerequisites

Before real participation:

- named accountable roles;
- conflicts and qualifications recorded;
- protocol approved for human use;
- live evidence environment verified;
- information, consent, refusal and withdrawal materials;
- privacy, retention, access and deletion controls;
- distress and incident response;
- accessibility and alternative participation;
- no marketing, model training, sales or performance use;
- independent stop authority.

## 7. Completion gate

A review class is complete only when:

- real qualified reviewers or participants took part;
- exact source versions and requirements were used;
- positive, negative and minority views were recorded;
- Critical/High findings have disposition or explicit bounded deferral;
- evidence handling complied with approved controls;
- changes were synchronized across Markdown and JSON;
- an accountable decision maker signed the result.

A meeting, automated critique, repository check, or synthetic tabletop alone is
not completion.
