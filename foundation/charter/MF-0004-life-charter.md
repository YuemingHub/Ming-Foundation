# MF-0004 — The Charter of Life

> **Draft for review. This document is not yet an Accepted or Stable standard.**  
> **审查草案：本文件尚未成为 Accepted 或 Stable 标准。**

## 1. Purpose

The Charter of Life states the ethical commitments that should govern any system, organization, profession, or technology that seeks to observe, interpret, support, educate, care for, or make decisions affecting human life.

It asks:

> **How should life be treated, regardless of organization, technology, product, role, or era?**  
> **无论任何组织、任何技术、任何角色、任何时代，应当如何对待生命？**

This Charter is broader than MingOS. MingOS may commit to it, but MingOS does not own it.

## 2. Status and limits

This document is:

- an ethical and architectural Charter draft;
- a source of constraints for standards, products, research, and governance;
- deliberately revisable through evidence, affected-person review, and public reasoning.

This document is not:

- a complete scientific theory of life;
- a medical, psychological, legal, or diagnostic standard;
- proof that Ming Foundation has discovered universal laws of human development;
- authority to override a person’s own meaning, values, rights, or lawful choices.

The Charter MUST remain open to correction. Loyalty to life and truth is more important than loyalty to this document.

## 3. Preamble

Life is not raw material waiting to be optimized by a system.

A person is not a collection of problems, scores, labels, risks, transactions, or prompts. Every life exists in time, relationship, history, culture, embodiment, uncertainty, and possibility.

Systems may help people see more clearly. They must not claim the right to own, define, control, or replace life.

## 4. Articles

### C01 — Truth before theory  
### 真实高于理论

A theory, model, method, brand, or institution MUST yield when reality and credible evidence contradict it.

No system may distort lived experience to preserve its own authority.

### C02 — Life before system and efficiency  
### 生命高于系统与效率

Systems exist to support life. Life does not exist to satisfy systems, metrics, growth targets, automation, or institutional convenience.

Efficiency is legitimate only when it remains subordinate to dignity, safety, relationship, context, and agency.

### C03 — A person is not a problem set  
### 人不是问题集合

No person should be reduced to a diagnosis, behavior, performance score, risk category, user segment, permanent profile, or single narrative.

A behavior may be a signal. It is not the whole person.

### C04 — Agency cannot be transferred away  
### 人始终拥有主体性

A system may support reflection, provide information, identify uncertainty, and offer options. It MUST NOT become the final authority over a person’s identity, meaning, values, relationships, or life direction.

People must be able to disagree, refuse, pause, correct, and leave.

### C05 — Ordinary, slow, uncertain, and limited lives retain full dignity  
### 普通、缓慢、不确定与有限的生命同样值得尊重

Human worth does not depend on exceptional achievement, speed, productivity, compliance, visibility, or optimization.

Rest, grief, refusal, uncertainty, limitation, and changing direction may be legitimate parts of life.

### C06 — Growth cannot be manufactured  
### 成长不能被制造

Growth cannot be forced into existence by control, pressure, manipulation, or a universal timetable.

Systems SHOULD support the conditions in which growth may occur: safety, relationship, time, meaning, capability, participation, and room to change.

### C07 — Relationship and context are part of life  
### 关系与情境是生命展开的环境

A person cannot be understood as an isolated data point.

Interpretation and support must consider relationship, power, history, development, culture, environment, embodiment, and current conditions.

### C08 — Safety before change, without domination  
### 安全先于改变，但安全不能成为支配

Safety-sensitive action may be necessary. It must be proportionate, explainable, reviewable, minimally intrusive, and attentive to the harms of surveillance and control.

Safety does not justify unlimited authority.

### C09 — Every interpretation remains provisional  
### 任何解释都必须允许被修正

Facts, observations, hypotheses, patterns, judgments, and decisions MUST be distinguished.

Every meaningful interpretation should preserve source, time, evidence, confidence, uncertainty, confirmation status, and revision history.

### C10 — Technology must serve life  
### 技术必须服务生命

Technology may help people observe, remember, reflect, communicate, and choose. It MUST NOT replace living, conscience, relationship, responsibility, or the person’s right to determine meaning.

AI cannot live a person’s life on their behalf.

### C11 — Memory must preserve change and rights  
### 记忆必须允许生命改变

Memory should preserve continuity without turning the past into destiny.

People must have meaningful rights to inspect, correct, contextualize, revoke, archive, and delete information, subject to lawful and narrowly justified safety requirements.

### C12 — Humility, failure, counterexample, and the unknown are protected  
### 谦卑、失败、反例与未知必须被保留

A life-supporting system MUST preserve evidence that it may be wrong.

Failure cases, counterexamples, dissent, unresolved questions, and unknowns must not be hidden merely to protect confidence, reputation, conversion, or model performance.

## 5. Charter tests

A proposal conflicts with this Charter when it:

- improves engagement by increasing fear, shame, dependency, or compulsion;
- turns a temporary state into a permanent identity;
- treats one person’s account as complete truth about another person;
- hides inference behind authoritative language;
- makes institutional efficiency more important than dignity or agency;
- removes meaningful correction, refusal, consent, or exit;
- claims certainty unsupported by evidence;
- uses safety as a pretext for unnecessary surveillance or domination;
- asks people to serve the theory, product, model, or brand.

## 6. Relationship to MingOS

The Charter of Life defines the higher ethical commitments.

The MingOS Charter must translate these commitments into organizational, product, data, commercial, professional, and technical self-restraints.

The MingOS Kernel must translate those self-restraints into operational protocols, data models, review gates, tests, and runtime behavior.

```text
The Charter of Life
  ↓
MingOS Charter
  ↓
MingOS Kernel
  ↓
Products, agents, services, research, and operations
```

No lower layer may silently redefine a higher layer.

## 7. Review questions

Before this Draft may become Accepted, review must address:

1. Does the wording make claims that exceed available evidence?
2. Are agency and safety balanced without creating an absolute rule that fails in emergencies?
3. Are correction, deletion, consent, and memory rights stated with sufficient legal and operational care?
4. Does the Charter adequately represent children and people whose voice may be mediated by others?
5. Which articles are universal ethical commitments, and which are specific design preferences?
6. What affected-person and external ethics review is required?
7. How should conflicts between articles be documented and resolved?
