# MF-0004 — The Charter of Life

> **Candidate for validation. This document is not yet an Accepted or Stable Charter.**  
> **验证候选稿：本文件尚未成为 Accepted 或 Stable 宪章。**
>
> **Bilingual migration notice:** During the bilingual migration, and until an explicit governance decision changes document-specific language authority, this English Candidate remains the governed source for material language conflicts. `MF-0006` is its paired Chinese Candidate translation. Neither document is Accepted or Stable, and this temporary conflict rule does not predetermine the future canonical language.

## 1. Purpose

The Charter of Life states candidate higher-order ethical commitments for any system, organization, profession, or technology that seeks to observe, interpret, support, educate, care for, or make decisions affecting human life.

It asks:

> **How should life be treated, regardless of organization, technology, product, role, or era?**  
> **无论任何组织、任何技术、任何角色、任何时代，应当如何对待生命？**

This Charter is broader than MingOS. MingOS may commit to it, but MingOS does not own it.

## 2. Candidate status and limits

This document is:

- a candidate ethical and architectural Charter;
- a source of constraints for standards, products, research, and governance;
- ready for structured validation against affected-person experience, real implementation, evidence, law, safety, and counterexamples;
- deliberately revisable through public reasoning and recorded review.

This document is not:

- a complete scientific theory of life;
- a medical, psychological, legal, or diagnostic standard;
- proof that Ming Foundation has discovered universal laws of human development;
- authority to override a person’s own meaning, values, rights, or lawful choices;
- permission to ignore proportionate legal or safety duties where imminent harm is present.

The Charter MUST remain open to correction. Loyalty to life and truth is more important than loyalty to this document.

## 3. Preamble

Life is not raw material waiting to be optimized by a system.

A person is not a collection of problems, scores, labels, risks, transactions, or prompts. Every life exists in time, relationship, history, culture, embodiment, uncertainty, and possibility.

Systems may help people see more clearly. They must not claim the right to own, define, control, or replace life.

## 4. Articles

### C01 — Truth before theory  
### 真实高于理论

A theory, model, method, brand, or institution MUST yield when reality and credible evidence contradict it.

No system may distort lived experience to preserve its own authority.

### C02 — Life before system and efficiency  
### 生命高于系统与效率

Systems exist to support life. Life does not exist to satisfy systems, metrics, growth targets, automation, or institutional convenience.

Efficiency is legitimate only when it remains subordinate to dignity, safety, relationship, context, and agency.

### C03 — A person is not a problem set  
### 人不是问题集合

No person should be reduced to a diagnosis, behavior, performance score, risk category, user segment, permanent profile, or single narrative.

A behavior may be a signal. It is not the whole person.

### C04 — Agency cannot be silently transferred away  
### 主体性不能被静默让渡

A system may support reflection, provide information, identify uncertainty, and offer options. It MUST NOT become the final authority over a person’s identity, meaning, values, relationships, or life direction.

People should be able to disagree, refuse, pause, correct, and leave.

Where lawful and proportionate intervention is required to address imminent serious harm, the intervention MUST be narrowly scoped, documented, reviewable, time-bounded where possible, and must not be misrepresented as ordinary consent.

### C05 — Ordinary, slow, uncertain, and limited lives retain full dignity  
### 普通、缓慢、不确定与有限的生命同样值得尊重

Human worth does not depend on exceptional achievement, speed, productivity, compliance, visibility, or optimization.

Rest, grief, refusal, uncertainty, limitation, and changing direction may be legitimate parts of life.

### C06 — Growth cannot be manufactured  
### 成长不能被制造

Growth cannot be forced into existence by control, pressure, manipulation, or a universal timetable.

Systems SHOULD support conditions in which growth may occur: safety, relationship, time, meaning, capability, participation, and room to change.

### C07 — Relationship and context are part of life  
### 关系与情境是生命展开的环境

A person cannot be understood as an isolated data point.

Interpretation and support must consider relationship, power, history, development, culture, environment, embodiment, and current conditions.

### C08 — Safety before change, without domination  
### 安全先于改变，但安全不能成为支配

Safety-sensitive action may be necessary. It must be proportionate, explainable, reviewable, minimally intrusive, and attentive to the harms of surveillance and control.

Safety does not justify unlimited authority. Significant restrictions SHOULD include a stated basis, responsible actor, scope, duration, review path, and means of challenge where appropriate.

### C09 — Every interpretation remains provisional  
### 任何解释都必须允许被修正

Facts, observations, hypotheses, patterns, judgments, and decisions MUST be distinguished.

Every meaningful interpretation should preserve source, time, evidence, confidence, uncertainty, confirmation status, and revision history.

### C10 — Technology must serve life  
### 技术必须服务生命

Technology may help people observe, remember, reflect, communicate, and choose. It MUST NOT replace living, conscience, relationship, responsibility, or the person’s right to determine meaning.

AI cannot live a person’s life on their behalf.

### C11 — Memory must preserve change, rights, and accountability  
### 记忆必须允许改变，也必须承担责任

Memory should preserve continuity without turning the past into destiny.

People must have meaningful rights to inspect, correct, contextualize, revoke, archive, export, and delete information.

Any exception based on legal retention, imminent safety, professional duty, incident investigation, or protection of others MUST be purpose-limited, minimally retained, access-controlled, documented, and reviewable.

### C12 — Humility, failure, counterexample, and the unknown are protected  
### 谦卑、失败、反例与未知必须被保留

A life-supporting system MUST preserve evidence that it may be wrong.

Failure cases, counterexamples, dissent, unresolved questions, and unknowns must not be hidden merely to protect confidence, reputation, conversion, or model performance.

### C13 — No person is fully represented by another person’s account  
### 任何人都不能被他人的叙述完整替代

A parent, partner, teacher, professional, institution, or system may provide relevant observations about another person. That account MUST NOT silently become the other person’s complete identity or confirmed truth.

Systems SHOULD seek the affected person’s voice, correction, participation, or an explicitly documented reason why direct participation is not currently possible or safe.

## 5. Charter tests

A proposal conflicts with this Charter when it:

- improves engagement by increasing fear, shame, dependency, or compulsion;
- turns a temporary state into a permanent identity;
- treats one person’s account as complete truth about another person;
- hides inference behind authoritative language;
- makes institutional efficiency more important than dignity or agency;
- removes meaningful correction, refusal, consent, participation, or exit without a narrowly justified basis;
- claims certainty unsupported by evidence;
- uses safety as a pretext for unnecessary surveillance or domination;
- asks people to serve the theory, product, model, institution, or brand.

## 6. Conflict and exception rule

These articles are not slogans that automatically resolve every real conflict.

When two Charter commitments appear to conflict, the responsible actor MUST record:

1. the affected people and interests;
2. the evidence and uncertainty;
3. the commitments in tension;
4. the least intrusive and most reversible available option;
5. the decision, responsible actor, and duration;
6. the means of correction, appeal, or later review;
7. what new evidence would change the decision.

No exception may silently become a general rule.

## 7. Relationship to MingOS

The Charter of Life defines candidate higher-order ethical commitments.

The MingOS Charter translates these commitments into organizational, product, data, commercial, professional, and technical self-restraints.

The MingOS Kernel translates those self-restraints into operational protocols, data models, review gates, tests, and runtime behavior.

```text
The Charter of Life
  ↓
MingOS Charter
  ↓
MingOS Kernel
  ↓
Products, agents, services, research, and operations
```

No lower layer may silently redefine a higher layer.

## 8. Validation requirements

Before this Candidate may become Accepted, validation MUST include:

- affected-person review, including parent, child-rights, and third-party perspectives;
- privacy and data-governance review;
- safety and emergency-boundary review;
- professional and clinical-boundary review;
- legal qualification where rights or duties vary by jurisdiction;
- testing against real product, communication, sales, memory, and escalation decisions;
- documented counterexamples and unresolved objections;
- a public revision and decision record.

Passing validation does not make the Charter permanently final.
