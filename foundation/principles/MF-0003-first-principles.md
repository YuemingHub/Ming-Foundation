# First Principles

## P01 — Life before system

The system exists to support life. Life does not exist to satisfy the system.

A feature, metric, or model that improves performance while degrading dignity, safety, relationship, or agency is a system failure.

## P02 — Understanding before advice

Advice without context can become noise, pressure, or harm.

The system should first clarify the event, participants, context, emotion, need, relationship, uncertainty, and the user’s own intention.

## P03 — Relationship before method

Human change is shaped by relationship. Techniques cannot be evaluated independently of trust, safety, power, history, and connection.

Relationship is therefore a core data dimension, not an optional module.

## P04 — Growth is not optimization

Growth may involve rest, grief, uncertainty, refusal, slowing down, changing direction, or accepting limits.

Ming Foundation rejects the assumption that growth always means more output, speed, compliance, or measurable performance.

## P05 — Every interpretation is revisable

No profile, pattern, state, or conclusion is final.

Systems must preserve:

- evidence;
- confidence;
- time;
- source;
- user confirmation;
- revision history.

## P06 — Human agency is non-transferable

A system may support reflection and offer options. It may not become the final authority over a person’s identity, meaning, values, relationships, or life direction.

## P07 — Distinguish fact from inference

Facts, observations, hypotheses, patterns, judgments, and decisions must be represented differently.

The system must not hide inference behind authoritative language.

## P08 — Consent is continuous

Consent is not a single checkbox. It is specific, informed, revocable, time-aware, and purpose-bound.

## P09 — Memory must allow change

Memory should preserve continuity without turning history into destiny.

People must be able to inspect, correct, contextualize, archive, and delete data according to applicable rights and safety constraints.

## P10 — Safety without domination

Safety mechanisms are necessary, but must be proportional, explainable, and designed to avoid unnecessary surveillance or control.

## P11 — Capability over dependency

A healthy system should gradually strengthen the user’s capacity to understand, choose, communicate, and act.

Retention created by dependency is not success.

## P12 — Humility is a system requirement

Uncertainty is not a user-interface decoration. It is part of the data model, reasoning process, governance, and audit trail.
