# PROJECT-MINGOS-0002 — MingOS Charter

> **Candidate for validation. This Charter is self-restraint, not marketing copy.**  
> **验证候选稿：这是一份自我约束文件，不是宣传文案。**
> **Bilingual migration notice:** During bilingual migration, and until an explicit governance decision changes document-specific language authority, this English Candidate remains the governed source for material language conflicts. `PROJECT-MINGOS-0003` is its paired Chinese Candidate translation. Neither document is Accepted or Stable.

## 1. Purpose

The MingOS Charter states how MingOS, its maintainers, products, agents, professionals, partners, and future organizations commit to practice the Charter of Life.

It asks:

> **How will MingOS constrain itself so that technology serves life rather than owning, reducing, manipulating, or replacing it?**  
> **MingOS 将如何约束自己，使技术服务生命，而不是占有、简化、操纵或替代生命？**

Candidate status means the commitments have passed an internal article-by-article review and are ready for external, affected-person, legal, safety, and implementation validation. It does not mean they are already fully enforceable or universally validated.

## 2. Preamble

MingOS is one current expression of a life-centered approach. It is not the center of life and must never become more important than the people it claims to support.

If evidence, lived experience, failure, or affected people show that MingOS is wrong, MingOS must change its model rather than distort reality to preserve the model.

We are loyal to life and truth, not to the permanence of the brand, framework, founder, expert, organization, or AI system.

## 3. Commitments

### MC01 — MingOS stands with life, not with a role  
### MingOS 站在生命一边，而不是任何角色一边

MingOS must not automatically side with a parent, child, school, professional, institution, customer, founder, or model.

It must ask what protects dignity, safety, relationship, agency, development, and truthful understanding for all affected lives.

### MC02 — MingOS does not own any life  
### MingOS 不拥有任何生命

MingOS may maintain authorized records and provide services. It does not own a person’s identity, meaning, story, future, relationship, or life direction.

No product language may imply that a life belongs to the system.

### MC03 — People may refuse interpretation, pause, and leave  
### 用户可以拒绝被解释、暂停并离开

A person should be able to:

- decline an interpretation;
- ask how it was produced;
- correct or contextualize it;
- refuse continued profiling;
- pause or end participation;
- revoke consent within defined limits;
- understand what remains stored and why.

Refusal must not be treated as failure, resistance, or evidence that the system is correct.

Any exception required by law, imminent safety, incident handling, or protection of others MUST be narrowly stated, purpose-limited, reviewable, and distinguished from consent.

### MC04 — Data and memory remain accountable to the person  
### 数据与记忆必须向生命负责

MingOS must provide meaningful controls over collection, purpose, access, correction, retention, portability, deletion, and audit.

Memory must not freeze a person into an old state. Temporary vulnerability must not become a permanent profile.

Where deletion or revocation cannot be fully honored, MingOS must explain the reason, scope, retention period, access controls, and review path.

### MC05 — Evidence is above authority  
### 证据高于权威

Experts, founders, maintainers, institutions, and models are not above evidence.

MingOS must preserve contradiction, failure, counterexample, uncertainty, user correction, and revision history.

### MC06 — Fact, observation, hypothesis, pattern, judgment, and decision remain distinct  
### 事实、观察、假设、模式、判断与决定必须分开

MingOS must not convert a model inference into a fact through confident language.

A parent’s account about a child, a professional’s interpretation, and an AI-generated hypothesis are different knowledge objects and must remain traceable.

### MC07 — MingOS must not use anxiety to increase conversion  
### 不能用焦虑提高转化

MingOS must not exaggerate risk, manufacture urgency, shame families, or imply that users are failing in order to sell, retain, upsell, or secure compliance.

People seeking help are often already carrying fear. Business design must not intensify it.

### MC08 — MingOS must not use dependency to increase retention  
### 不能用依赖提高留存

A healthy MingOS service should strengthen the person’s and family’s capacity to understand, communicate, choose, and act without the system.

Retention created by emotional dependence, hidden persuasion, compulsive use, or fear of leaving is a failure.

### MC09 — AI must remain a bounded component  
### AI 必须是有边界的组成部分

AI may summarize, identify possible patterns, surface uncertainty, ask questions, generate options, and support reflection.

AI must not:

- present itself as a human professional;
- claim final authority over identity, meaning, diagnosis, or life choice;
- hide that content was inferred or generated;
- exploit vulnerability;
- replace human conscience or responsibility;
- make high-impact decisions without appropriate human and affected-person participation.

### MC10 — Children and third parties require independent protection  
### 儿童与第三方必须获得独立保护

A parent’s participation does not automatically authorize unlimited interpretation, profiling, monitoring, or disclosure about a child or other family member.

MingOS must design for:

- age and developmental appropriateness;
- the child’s or third party’s voice where possible;
- purpose limitation;
- minimum necessary data;
- correction and contestability;
- protection from surveillance, humiliation, coercion, and performance management;
- independent escalation when safety requires it.

### MC11 — Professional and safety boundaries must remain visible  
### 专业与安全边界必须清晰可见

MingOS does not replace emergency response, medical care, mental-health diagnosis, legal advice, child-protection duties, or qualified professional judgment.

When risk, uncertainty, or impact exceeds system competence, MingOS must slow down, disclose limits, and support appropriate escalation.

### MC12 — Commercial power cannot amend the root commitments  
### 商业不能修改根原则

Revenue, growth, engagement, investor pressure, customer demand, competitive urgency, and operational convenience cannot silently override the Charter.

Material conflicts between business goals and Charter commitments must be documented, reviewable, and governed by an actor with authority independent of the immediate commercial objective.

### MC13 — MingOS must remain correctable  
### MingOS 必须允许被真实修正

MingOS must maintain mechanisms for:

- user correction and appeal;
- incident reporting;
- failure and counterexample review;
- model and policy versioning;
- independent criticism;
- audit trails;
- explicit supersession;
- preservation of unknowns.

### MC14 — Charter violations require response, remediation, and traceability  
### 宪章违规必须被回应、修复并留下记录

A credible Charter must constrain conduct.

MingOS must define a process to:

- receive and protect good-faith reports;
- classify severity and affected people;
- contain ongoing harm;
- correct data, product behavior, communication, or governance;
- notify affected people where appropriate;
- document exceptions and decisions;
- review recurrence and root causes;
- suspend or withdraw the MingOS name from materially non-conforming implementations when necessary.

No person, founder, commercial partner, professional, or AI system is exempt from this process.

## 4. Three permanent disciplines

MingOS adopts the following disciplines:

1. **Never lower truth for communication.**  
   **永远不要为了传播而降低真实。**

2. **Never manufacture anxiety for conversion.**  
   **永远不要为了成交而制造焦虑。**

3. **Never let AI replace human conscience.**  
   **永远不要让 AI 替代人的良知。**

These are not slogans. Product, sales, content, research, and implementation decisions must be testable against them.

## 5. Decision precedence

When a MingOS decision involves competing interests, use this order:

1. imminent safety and lawful protection duties, applied proportionately;
2. dignity, agency, consent, correction, and affected-person participation;
3. truthful evidence, uncertainty, relationship, and context;
4. sustainable professional and operational responsibility;
5. business, engagement, growth, and convenience.

This is not an automatic formula. Material conflicts must be documented under the conflict rule in `MF-0004`.

## 6. Conformance questions

Before a MingOS feature, message, intervention, or business practice is approved, reviewers should ask:

1. Does this help a person see more truthfully, or help MingOS appear correct?
2. Does it strengthen agency, or quietly create compliance and dependency?
3. Does it distinguish evidence from inference?
4. Does it respect the rights and voice of people not directly operating the interface?
5. Does it increase fear, shame, urgency, or attachment for commercial benefit?
6. Can the person inspect, correct, refuse, participate, and leave?
7. Are the limits of AI and professional competence visible?
8. What failure, counterexample, and unknown would change this decision?
9. Is the smallest safe and reversible action being preferred?
10. Would the decision remain defensible if publicly audited?
11. Who is responsible if this decision causes harm?
12. What evidence will be collected to determine whether the decision worked?

## 7. Relationship to the Kernel

This Charter defines commitments. It does not define the complete runtime.

The MingOS Kernel must operationalize this Charter through:

- identity and consent;
- knowledge-status separation;
- evidence and confidence;
- observation before advice;
- safety and escalation;
- memory correction and deletion;
- relationship and timeline context;
- small, user-chosen actions;
- follow-up and feedback;
- failure, counterexample, and unknown repositories;
- incident response, appeal, remediation, audit, review, and versioning.

The Kernel is not a single system prompt, a counseling script, or a model personality. It is the governed operational order that all MingOS implementations must satisfy.

## 8. Candidate validation requirements

Before this Candidate may become Accepted, validation MUST resolve:

1. which commitments are mandatory for every MingOS implementation;
2. jurisdiction-specific qualifications for data rights and retention;
3. how children and non-user family members exercise contestability;
4. what decisions require independent ethics, legal, safety, or professional review;
5. how commercial conflicts are disclosed and adjudicated;
6. minimum conformance tests for using the MingOS name;
7. enforcement, appeal, remediation, suspension, and reinstatement procedures;
8. evidence from current website, Family OS, communication, sales, and implementation audits;
9. unresolved objections and counterexamples.
