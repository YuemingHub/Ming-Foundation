# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Ming Foundation exists to support the dignity, agency, safety, and diversity of life. Participation in this project therefore requires more than technical civility: it requires care in how people and lived experiences are represented.

## Expected behavior

Participants should:

- engage with respect and intellectual honesty;
- distinguish evidence from interpretation;
- acknowledge uncertainty and limitations;
- avoid diagnostic, stigmatizing, or dehumanizing language;
- protect confidential and sensitive information;
- welcome correction from people affected by a design;
- disclose conflicts of interest;
- challenge ideas without attacking people.

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment, discrimination, threats, or humiliation;
- publishing identifiable personal or family data without explicit authorization;
- presenting unverified psychological or medical claims as fact;
- using vulnerable stories for promotion without meaningful consent;
- designing for coercion, dependency, manipulation, or hidden surveillance;
- retaliating against good-faith dissent or safety reports.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may warn, restrict, remove, or ban participation when behavior conflicts with these commitments. Safety-sensitive concerns should be handled privately and with minimal disclosure.

## Scope

This code applies to repository activity, project discussions, community spaces, events, and official communications.
