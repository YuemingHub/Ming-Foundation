# GOV-0006 — Charter Candidate Validation Plan

## 1. Purpose

This plan defines the evidence required before the Charter of Life or MingOS Charter may move from `Candidate` to `Accepted`.

The purpose is to test the Charters against reality rather than reward polished wording.

## 2. Validation streams

### V1 — Internal consistency

Check:

- Mission, Vision, First Principles, and Architecture Blueprint;
- duplicate or conflicting obligations;
- undefined terms;
- conflicts between higher-order and project-specific language;
- status and dependency consistency.

### V2 — Affected-person review

Include perspectives of:

- parents using family-support services;
- adolescents or child-rights representatives;
- people described by another user;
- professionals and non-professional supporters;
- people who disagree with or refuse system interpretations;
- people affected by safety escalation.

Raw identifiable cases MUST NOT enter the public repository.

### V3 — Privacy and data governance

Test:

- collection and purpose limitation;
- consent and revocation;
- correction and contestability;
- memory revision;
- deletion and retention exceptions;
- third-party data;
- audit access;
- de-identification and publication boundaries.

### V4 — Safety and professional boundaries

Test:

- imminent-harm exceptions;
- crisis and emergency escalation;
- clinical, medical, legal, and child-protection boundaries;
- proportionality and least-intrusive action;
- human review and responsibility;
- documentation and appeal.

### V5 — Commercial and communication audit

Audit:

- `mingos.cn`;
- product onboarding;
- marketing, sales, and conversion language;
- retention and re-engagement mechanics;
- pricing and upselling;
- claims about AI, professionals, outcomes, and completion.

No claim should turn a vision into a represented current capability.

### V6 — Implementation evidence

Map Charter commitments against:

- current Ming Family / Family OS behavior;
- prompts and response protocols;
- memory and user-profile behavior;
- safety gates;
- case and feedback records;
- failure, counterexample, and unknown repositories;
- audit and versioning mechanisms.

Implementation is evidence, not automatic authority.

### V7 — Counterexample and red-team review

Actively search for:

- cases where autonomy language could create harm;
- cases where safety language could justify control;
- conflicts between privacy and protection of others;
- manipulation hidden behind supportive language;
- dependence presented as companionship;
- permanent profiling from temporary vulnerability;
- commercial conflicts;
- situations not represented by family-education assumptions.

### V8 — Legal and jurisdiction review

Identify statements requiring jurisdiction-specific qualification.

The repository may state principles, but MUST NOT present unreviewed legal wording as universal legal fact.

## 3. Evidence record

Each validation finding should record:

- validation stream;
- Charter article or commitment;
- scenario or evidence;
- source classification;
- affected people;
- finding;
- severity;
- proposed change;
- responsible reviewer;
- status;
- decision and rationale;
- public or restricted handling.

## 4. Acceptance threshold

A Charter may become `Accepted` only when:

- every article has been tested in all applicable streams;
- high-severity conflicts have a documented resolution;
- unresolved legal and safety limits are clearly qualified;
- affected-person objections are represented rather than summarized away;
- no known material contradiction is hidden;
- implementation gaps are acknowledged;
- enforcement and appeal paths are sufficiently defined;
- the acceptance decision is recorded in a dedicated governance document.

`Stable` requires additional long-term evidence and is not a Day 5 target.

## 5. Failure conditions

Validation fails when:

- review is performed only by the founder or drafting AI;
- no child-rights or third-party perspective is represented;
- implementation reality is ignored;
- exceptions are hidden;
- marketing pressure changes wording without governance;
- lack of evidence is presented as consensus;
- open objections are omitted from the public record without a privacy or safety basis.

## 6. Day 5 target

Day 5 should produce a Charter validation evidence pack, not a new theoretical layer.

Recommended outputs:

- website and public-claim audit;
- Family OS implementation mapping;
- privacy and third-party rights gap analysis;
- safety and professional-boundary gap analysis;
- counterexample register;
- status recommendation for each Candidate Charter.

Day 5 may recommend acceptance, revision, or continued Candidate status.
