# GOV-TPL-0010 — Child-Friendly Profile Review Guide

## Use boundary

Use only under an approved safeguarding, consent, assent, accessibility, and
independent-facilitation process.

Do not ask for personal case, crisis, medical, legal, school, or family
secrets.

## Opening

- This is about whether some rules are understandable and fair.
- There are no right answers.
- You may skip, stop, ask for help, or change your mind.
- Saying no will not cause a penalty.
- We are not deciding anything about you.

## Review prompts

1. What do you think the rule is saying?
2. Could a person say “I do not understand”?
3. Could they disagree without being called difficult, immature, unsafe, or
   incapable?
4. Do P0–P5 look like a score about the person?
5. Who should be allowed to speak for a child?
6. What should happen when the child and adult disagree?
7. What information should never be shared without explaining why?
8. What would make the words easier to understand?

## Stop rule

Stop immediately if the participant is distressed, feels pressured, starts
disclosing a sensitive live situation, or asks to stop.

Record only the minimum process and safeguarding information required by the
approved protocol.
