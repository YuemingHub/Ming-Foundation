## Review tracks

1. child-friendly comprehension and agency;
2. adolescent autonomy, privacy, and contestability;
3. parent, caregiver, and representative;
4. family, education, mental-health, and safeguarding practitioner;
5. privacy, data-rights, and safety;
6. accessibility, language, and communication;
7. jurisdiction and professional duty.

## Entry gates

No track may begin until it has:

- a named safeguarding and review owner;
- approved consent and assent;
- a non-coercive compensation rule;
- a data-minimized evidence plan;
- withdrawal and deletion routes;
- accessibility and language support;
- a distress, disclosure, and stop protocol;
- restricted evidence storage;
- an independent facilitator where appropriate.

## Prohibition

General review instruments must not solicit identifiable child, crisis,
medical, psychiatric, legal, immigration, financial, school, or third-party
case narratives.

A separate approved process is required for any sensitive evidence.
