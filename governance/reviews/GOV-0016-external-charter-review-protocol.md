# GOV-0016 — External Charter Review Protocol

## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines how Ming Foundation should request, receive,
preserve, and act on external review of the Candidate Charters and
remediation protocols.

It prevents “external review” from meaning informal endorsement by
friendly experts.

## 2. Review domains

The review program should include independent perspectives in:

- child rights and youth participation;
- privacy and data governance;
- safety, crisis, and safeguarding;
- mental-health and professional boundaries;
- family systems and education;
- AI governance and human-computer interaction;
- commercial ethics and consumer protection;
- law in relevant operating jurisdictions;
- accessibility and inclusion;
- lived or affected experience.

One reviewer is not expected to cover every domain.

## 3. Independence requirements

Each reviewer must disclose:

- financial relationship;
- employment or advisory relationship;
- prior authorship;
- personal relationship with project leadership;
- commercial interest;
- advocacy or institutional position;
- confidentiality constraints.

Compensation may pay for time. It MUST NOT depend on endorsement or
approval.

## 4. Review package

A reviewer should receive:

- Candidate Charters;
- `ADR-0005` and `ADR-0006`;
- Day 5 evidence and gap records;
- Day 6 Proposed RFCs relevant to the reviewer;
- counterexample register;
- evidence limitations;
- explicit questions;
- response template;
- confidentiality and publication options.

The package must distinguish verified evidence from documentary claims.

## 5. Review questions

Every external reviewer should address:

1.  Which commitments are necessary and clear?
2.  Which commitments are overbroad, misleading, culturally narrow, or
    unenforceable?
3.  Which people or harms are missing?
4.  Where could agency language create unsafe inaction?
5.  Where could safety language justify control or surveillance?
6.  Which data or child-rights commitments are incomplete?
7.  Which public claims would be misleading?
8.  Which implementation evidence is insufficient?
9.  What would prevent acceptance?
10. Which dissent should be preserved even if the text is not changed?

## 6. Permitted conclusions

A reviewer may conclude:

- support;
- support with conditions;
- revise;
- split;
- move to another layer;
- reject;
- unable to determine;
- conflict prevents review.

A project summary MUST NOT convert conditional or partial support into
endorsement.

## 7. Handling disagreement

Ming Foundation must preserve:

- the reviewer’s actual conclusion;
- material objections;
- project response;
- accepted and rejected changes;
- unresolved disagreement;
- confidentiality basis where full publication is unsafe.

A majority vote does not erase a serious minority objection.

## 8. Publication

Reviewers choose one of:

- public attribution and full response;
- public attribution and summarized response;
- anonymous public summary;
- restricted response;
- no publication beyond governance team.

The project should publish the strongest safe level allowed by the
reviewer.

## 9. Decision integration

Each review finding should become a `GOV-TPL-0003` record.

The project response must state:

- accepted;
- partially accepted;
- rejected;
- deferred;
- no action;
- further evidence required.

Reasons are mandatory.

## 10. Completion threshold

External review preparation is complete when:

- reviewer domains and conflicts are documented;
- the package is frozen to a commit;
- response and finding templates exist;
- restricted evidence handling is in force;
- publication options are clear;
- no invitation promises endorsement language.
